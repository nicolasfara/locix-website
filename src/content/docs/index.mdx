## A Shared Foundation

**Distributed systems are easier to build when the right abstractions can work together.**
Real applications often combine **architectural placement**, **protocol coordination**, and *neighborhood-style collective behavior*, and {frameworkName} lets you bring those styles together in **one cohesive model**.
By building on **capabilities** and **placement types**, {frameworkName} helps you compose them naturally while keeping boundaries **clear**, interactions **safe**, and the overall design *easier to reason about*.
<Foundations />

## Safety Features

{frameworkName} keeps **placement explicit**, **capabilities scoped**, and **cross-paradigm handoffs typed**, so composition preserves clear boundaries instead of weakening guarantees.
That means values remain attached to the peers that own them, paradigm-specific operations are only available inside the right context, and data or behavior **cannot silently cross locations** without an explicit typed step.
Instead of discovering mistakes later as *protocol bugs*, *invalid captures*, or *misplaced computations* at runtime, you get **direct feedback from the compiler** at the exact point where the boundary is being violated.
Scala's type system and capture checking make these guarantees practical to express and enforce at compile time, letting you combine **multitier**, **choreographic**, and **collective** code with stronger safety and *far less defensive boilerplate*.
