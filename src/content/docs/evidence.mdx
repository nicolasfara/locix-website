## What Is Directly Supported By The Paper

### Core evidence

- The submitted paper evaluates **46** use cases drawn from multitier, choreographic, and aggregate programming literature.
- The paper reports **3,874 lines of framework code** across those reimplementations, broken down as **2,012** choreography, **1,425** multitier, and **437** aggregate.
- The paper presents a formal core and type-soundness argument for the interaction of placed values and cross-paradigm operations.
- The paper frames the work around three research questions: expressiveness and safety, inter-paradigm integration, and portability to other capability mechanisms and languages.

### Integrated case studies discussed in the paper

- **CityEvt** combines user registration, recommendation delivery, and crowd monitoring across smartphones, edge nodes, and cloud.
- **SmrtMob** combines collective traffic sensing, edge-side coordination, and route recommendation.
- **Nebula** is presented as a port of an open-source federated learning platform, focusing on round coordination, topology-aware participation, and dashboard reporting.

### Nebula proof points worth repeating carefully

The strongest dissemination angle for the Nebula port is semantic clarity, not operational completeness. The public porting notes in the repo argue that the MpaCT version is easier to read as a protocol specification because it replaces callback-heavy orchestration with direct control flow and typed communication terms. The same notes also state clearly what is *not* preserved: deployment concerns, retries, forwarding, dynamic runtime roles, and general framework infrastructure.

That distinction matters for credibility. Public messaging should say that the port improves specification clarity and reduces incidental control-flow complexity, not that it replaces the original system as a production framework.

## Artifact Status

The paper’s data-availability statement describes the artifact as a research snapshot rather than a polished archival release. The site should preserve that honesty. Promote the work as a serious research artifact with runnable examples and strong ideas, but avoid implying turnkey production readiness or finished packaging work that is not yet in the public repo.

## Guidance For Public Claims

1. Use paper-backed numbers exactly as stated on this page.
2. When talking about CityEvt or Nebula publicly, distinguish clearly between what is described in the paper and what is directly visible in the public repository today.
3. Avoid turning research evidence into product-marketing claims such as performance superiority or operational completeness unless new measurements are added publicly.
